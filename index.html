Building a Pinhole Camera

Setup

        The basis of our pinhole camera started with an 19x31x11 cm shoebox, which was lined with black paper on all sides except one, which was lined with white. We created an external sliding panel on the side opposite of the white-lined inside, which allowed us to easily switch between four apertures of 0.5mm, 1mm, 2mm, and 5mm. The camera used was the Honor 8 main camera, which features full exposure, ISO, and focus adjustment, all equivalent to that of a modern DSLR camera. We set a black pillar on the inside of the box so that the phone could rest inside easily while the photo was taken. Tape was applied over the box accordingly to ensure that it was completely impermeable to light, aside from the sliding panel pinholes.
